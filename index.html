About

I am a microbiologist with a strong research focus on antimicrobial resistance (AMR), natural products, and infectious diseases. My academic training and research experience center on identifying biologically active microbial metabolites and understanding host–pathogen interactions that drive disease and treatment outcomes.

I hold a B.Sc. in Microbiology from the University of Ilorin, Nigeria, where I graduated with distinction and completed an undergraduate thesis on the antimicrobial potential and viability of endophytic fungi. I am currently engaged in collaborative research involving fungal secondary metabolites, biosynthetic pathway reactivation, and antimicrobial bioactivity.

I am seeking MSc and PhD training opportunities that combine experimental microbiology with translational approaches to drug discovery, delivery, and resistance mitigation.

Research Experience

My research experience spans laboratory-based microbiology, literature-driven discovery, and collaborative manuscript development. I have worked extensively with endophytic fungi, antimicrobial susceptibility testing, and metabolite extraction, while also contributing to computational and in silico analyses in drug discovery projects.

I have collaborated across microbiology and chemistry research teams on projects focused on fungal bioactivity, epigenetic modulation of secondary metabolite production, and the identification of candidate compounds targeting resistant bacterial pathogens.

In parallel, I actively engage in scientific writing and synthesis, contributing to peer-reviewed publications and review articles addressing antimicrobial resistance, fungal metabolites, and infectious disease challenges.
